Analysis

Analysis of current Indian agricultural situation

Area

Rice and wheat being the staple food of India it is being planted in large area. But still the production is not equal to the requirement. Like this many other crops also take too much space for cultivation but provides lower yield. To overcome this issue, a technology called vertical farming can be implemented.

Climate

The most optimum season for production is Kharif. Because of it's climatic conditions like temperature and humidity. These optimum conditions can be simulated in a controlled environment and this technique is called Controlled Environment Agriculture(CEA).

Years

The total requirement of food supply in India in 2012, 2013, was around 21.3 million tonnes but the production was just around 17.09 million tonnes, indicating a wide gap in demand and supply.

Methodology

Vertical Farming:

Vertical Farming is a novel method of growing crops by artificially stacking plants vertically above each other either in skyscrapers or by using the third dimension of space. This might help to solve many of future's problems like malnutrition, polluted food etc. that could become evident along with food scarcity.

Controlled Environment Agriculture:

Controlled Environment Agriculture (CEA) is an advanced and intensive form of hydroponically-based agriculture where plants grow within a controlled environment to optimize horticultural practices.
